Birthday Party Planning Tips

ğŸ“… Book Early

Popular birthday party spots fill up fast, especially on weekends. Reserve at least 2-3 weeks in advance, or even earlier during peak birthday season (summer and around holidays).

â° Choose the Right Time

For younger kids, lunch parties (11am-1pm) work best before nap time. For older kids, early dinner (4-6pm) or late lunch can be ideal. Weekend mornings are also great for brunch parties.

ğŸ“‹ Keep the Guest List Manageable

A good rule of thumb: age + 1 for younger kids (so 6 guests for a 5-year-old). This keeps things manageable and ensures everyone has a great time without overwhelming the restaurant.

ğŸ° Ask About Outside Cake

Most restaurants allow you to bring your own birthday cake, but some charge a cake-cutting fee ($10-25). Always call ahead to confirm their policy and ask if they provide plates, forks, and candles.

ğŸˆ Simplify Ordering

Pre-select 2-3 kid-friendly menu items for guests to choose from. This makes ordering faster and prevents analysis paralysis. Many restaurants offer party packages with set menus.

ğŸ’° Budget Wisely

Restaurant parties typically cost $15-30 per child including food and drinks. Factor in tax, tip (18-20% for larger parties), and any additional fees. Some restaurants require a deposit or minimum spend.
