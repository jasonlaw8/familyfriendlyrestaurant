Outdoor Dining Tips for Families

â˜€ï¸ Time It Right

Avoid the hottest parts of the day. Early lunch (11am-12pm) or early dinner (5-6pm) offer the most comfortable temperatures. Evening dining provides beautiful sunset views at many locations.

ğŸ§¥ Layer Up

Bay Area microclimates mean temperatures can vary greatly. Bring sweaters or light jackets even on sunny days. Many restaurants provide blankets, but it's best to come prepared.

ğŸª‘ Reserve Ahead

Popular outdoor patios fill up fast, especially on weekends and during nice weather. Make reservations and request outdoor seating specifically when you book.

ğŸ Manage Bugs & Sun

Bring kid-safe bug spray for evening dining. Choose shaded tables during peak sun hours. Many restaurants have fans or misters, but don't count on it.

ğŸš¸ Set Boundaries

Before sitting down, walk the patio with your kids and establish clear boundaries for where they can and cannot go. Point out any hazards like stairs or busy walkways.

ğŸ’ Pack Entertainment

Even outdoor spaces can have wait times for food. Bring quiet activities like coloring books or small toys. The scenery helps, but having a backup plan is smart.

Best Times for Outdoor Dining

Spring (March-May)

Perfect temperatures, blooming gardens. Can be rainy so check weather. Lunch and early dinner are ideal.

Summer (June-August)

Warmest season but often foggy near the coast. Inland areas great for evening dining. Bring layers.

Fall (September-November)

Bay Area's warmest season! Ideal for all-day outdoor dining. Book ahead as everyone has the same idea.

Winter (December-February)

Look for heated patios or covered areas. Sunny winter days can be lovely. Many spots offer fire pits and heaters.
